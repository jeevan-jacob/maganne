Orreries: Models of the Solar System

The History of Orreries

The concept of orreries has ancient roots, with devices like the Antikythera Mechanism laying the foundation for modern models.

The first modern orrery was built in the early 18th century by George Graham, named after Charles Boyle, the 4th Earl of Orrery.

100 BCE

The Antikythera Mechanism is created in ancient Greece.

1704

George Graham builds the first modern orrery.

How Orreries Work

Gears and Movement

Orreries use gears to model the precise movements of planets in orbit, driven by a central motor or hand crank.

Modeling the Solar System

Most orreries depict the Sun, Earth, and Moon system, with more complex models showing multiple planets and moons.

Modern Orreries

Today, orreries are used for education and art. Modern models blend traditional craftsmanship with cutting-edge technology.
